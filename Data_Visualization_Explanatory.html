An explanation of the variables used:

  • COUNTRY: 3 countries (Finland, United Arab Emirates, United States of America)
  • GENDER: 2 genders (Female, Male)
  • ENJMATH: answers to "Do you (the student) enjoy mathematics?" 4 answers (Strongly disagree, Disagree, Agree, Strongly agree)
  • PARENJMATH: answers to "Do your parents (the student's parents) enjoy mathematics?" 4 answers (Strongly disagree, Disagree, Agree, Strongly agree)
  • WEALTH: a float that is calculated by number of questions in the PISA 2012 dataset to measure wealth of the family of the student
  • TEACHERCLASSMANAG: a float that is calculated by number of questions in the PISA 2012 dataset to measure class management by the teacher
  • COGNITIVEACTIV: a float that is calculated by number of questions in the PISA 2012 dataset to measure the cognitive activation of mathematics by the student
  • TEACHSTUDRELAT: a float that is calculated by number of questions in the PISA 2012 dataset to measure the relationship between the teacher and the student
  • PV1MATH, PV2MATH, PV3MATH, PV4MATH, PV5MATH: 5 plausible values to the mathematical literacy of the students

The countries of interest for this report are Finland, United Arab Emirates, and United States of America. Finland is to reference the top education system of the world as it has been known. The UAE and USA are the two countries that I am interested in learning more about how their mathematical education perform with respect to each other and to Finland.

  • Note: Mathematical literacy is represented by plausible values of scores; you can learn more about this here.

plt.scatter(data=df, x='TEACHSTUDRELAT', y='TEACHERCLASSMANAG', c='COGNITIVEACTIV', cmap = 'mako_r', alpha= 0.5);

plt.colorbar(label = 'Cognitive Activation of Mathematics');
plt.title("Teacher's Class Management Versus Teacher-Student Relationship by Cognitive Activation of Mathematics", loc='center');
plt.xlabel('Teacher-Student Relationship');
plt.ylabel("Teacher's Class Management");

There is observations for this plot that helps understand how can a country improves it's student's cognitive activation of mathematics that in return increases mathematical literacy:

1) If teacher's class management is high and teacher-student relationship is low, and vice versa, the student's cognitive activation does not show a clear positive or negative relationship 2) If both teacher's class management and teacher-student relationship are high, then student's cognitive activation is high 3) If both teacher's class management and teacher-student relationship are low, then student's cognitive activation is low

Thus, we can recommend working on increasing the teacher's class management efforts and a better teacher-student relationship in order to increase the student's cognitive activation of mathematics, which in return increases the mathematical literacy of the students.
